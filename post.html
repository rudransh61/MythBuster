1+2+3.....

12-23-2023 - Rudransh Bharwdaj

There is a very famous mathematical argument that 1+2+3..... = -1/12=-0.08333333333333.. given by Ramanuja also known as "Ramanuja summation". But many people interpret this wrongly , For example a famous maths channel on youtube name "NumberPhile" proved this """correct""" statement wrongly . The speaker says that the Numberphile video made 3 wrong statements:

  • The infinite series 1 + 2 + 3 + ... actually sums to positive infinity, not -1/12.
  • The way the Numberphile video presents summing infinite series is not mathematically rigorous.
  • There is no such thing as “super summing” that can make divergent infinite series converge.
So , Lets deep dive into this problem and world of series in maths...........
A series is a type of sequence with meaningful order like 1,2,3,4,... or 1,3,5,7,9... or 2,3,5,7,11 or 4,9,16... etc. There is a long history of calculating sum of series and playing with them. And according to there sum , there are 2 types of series diverging and converging series
Diverging (sequence with sum tending to infinity ): From Medieval Latin dīvergō (“bend away from, go in a different direction”), from Latin dī- + vergō (“bend”). Converging (sequence with sum tending to some finite number ): traces back to the Latin word vergere, meaning “to bend or to turn.
